Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

"Father of Indonesian technology"

Here's a time line of B.J. Habibie's life:

  • 1936 - Born in Parepare, South Sulawesi
  • 1960 - Received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur
  • 1962 - Returned to Indonesia for three months on sick leave, married with Hasri Ainun, then returning to Germany
  • 1963 - Had a son, Ilham Akbar Habibie
  • 1965 - Delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing.)
  • 1974 - He was promoted to vice president of the company he worked, Messerschmitt-BÃ¶lkow-Blohm in Hamburg
  • 1978 - He was appointed as State Minister of Research and Technology (Indonesian: Menteri Negara Riset dan Teknologi, Menristek)
  • 1990 - While serving in cabinet, Habibie was also elected as the first Chair of the Indonesian Association of Muslim Intellectuals (ICMI) in 1990. This modernist Muslim organization provided him with a political base, linked to but independent of the Suharto administration
  • 1992 - Suharto appointed him as deputy daily coordinator for the chairman of the executive board
  • 1998 - Habibie was elected as Vice President just two months before succeded previous president as the Constitution says
  • 2006 - He released a book called Detik-Detik Yang Menentukan: Jalan Panjang Indonesia Menuju Demokrasi (Decisive Moments: Indonesia's Long Road Towards Democracy). The book recalled the events of May 1998 which led to his rise to the Presidency
  • 2019 - He was admitted to Gatot Soebroto Army Hospital, where he was undergoing treatments for heart problems, namely cardiomyopathy and died on 11 September 2019
